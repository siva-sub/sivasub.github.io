Delegated payment authority

An AI agent cannot just “pay”.

Before value moves, the system needs to know what the agent is allowed to do, which credential it may use, who absorbs failure, and when settlement is actually final.

The interesting question is not whether agents use cards, bank rails, or stablecoins. It is where the payment stack records delegated authority, and which institution is willing to stand behind it.

    Four-layer frame

    Design the delegation stack

    Use this as the article spine and the LinkedIn carousel structure. Each layer answers a different product and risk question.

    Authority

    Who instructed the agent, what scope was granted, and how can that scope be revoked?

    Credential

    Which credential is exposed to the agent, and is it single-use, merchant-bound, or wallet-bound?

    Liability

    Who owns fraud, unauthorised activity, refunds, disputes, compliance holds, and operational errors?

    Settlement

    When is the transfer final in the ledger, in law, and in the user’s practical recourse path?
