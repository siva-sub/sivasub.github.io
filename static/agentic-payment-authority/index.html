AI-agent payments

An AI agent cannot just “pay”.

Before it buys anything, the payment system has to decide what the agent is allowed to do, which credential it can use, and who carries the risk when something goes wrong.

Authority: who delegated the instruction, and under what limit?
Credential: card token, bank rail, or stablecoin wallet?
Liability: who owns fraud, refund, dispute, and compliance outcomes?
1
User instructionThe human asks the agent to act.
2
Agent policyLimits translate intent into permission.
3
Payment credentialThe system chooses how value moves.
4
Risk modelFraud, refunds, disputes, holds, and settlement.

The mechanism

Card token

Useful when acceptance, network rules, chargebacks, and fraud controls matter more than programmability.

Bank rail

Useful when regulated account movement, account ownership, and PSP controls are the centre of the payment.

Stablecoin wallet

Useful when programmable transfer and settlement-asset design matter, but redemption and compliance remain live questions.

Credential simulator

Card token

Best first fit when the agent needs existing merchant acceptance, spending controls, chargeback paths, and network fraud rules.

  • Set merchant category and amount caps before the credential is provisioned.
  • Use network token controls instead of exposing the underlying PAN.
  • Keep refund and chargeback paths visible to the user.
